Literary Books

Animal Farm

Writer: George Orwell

Animal Farm is an allegory regarding group of animals in a farm which they rises in opposition to their human owner who was a farmer as well in order for the purpose of establishment of a society of animals based on equity but as the time passes, the animals transition into becoming tyrannical and barbaric.

Macbeth

Writer: William Shakespeare

Macbeth is the most famous work of Sir William Shakespeare and revolves around several important themes such as goals, authority, and outcomes of a person's actions.

Merchant of Venice

Writer: William Shakespeare

The Merchant of Venice highlights the major themes of impartiality, forgiveness, and sterotypes etc. The story also involves the bond and trial of a merchant and a moneylender.
